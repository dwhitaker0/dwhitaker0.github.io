Dr. Darren Whitaker

Motivated reseacher with experience in method development of both vibrational and electronic spectroscopies for trace analysis and quantitative purposes. This includes extensive study and utilisation of infra-red, Raman and UV-Vis techniques in a number of environments.

Employment

Feb 2016 - Present; Senior Research Fellow in Chemometrics, Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Technology Centre @ Univeristy of Limerick, IRELAND

Interfacing with the pharmaceutical industry to enable the development of chemometrically driven soft sensor technology for use within the pharmaceutical manufacturing and development space.
I work specifically on data analysis techniques to extract useful information from messy process data, including spectroscopic and parametric measurements. This involves all parts of the process from designing mimetic processes on a lab scale to analysing GB's of acquired data. We have developed extensions to common algorithms and also implemented novel data analysis routines and methodologies.
This work has enabled me to improve my knowledge of proces chemistry, spectroscopy, chemical engineering and computer science. I am proficient in both python and R for instrument control and data analysis as well as familir with SQL and Postgre for the development of data storage and historian functionality.

Jan 2014 - Jan 2016; Postdoctoral Research Associate at IT Sligo, IRELAND

Working as part of a multinational and multidisciplinary team focussing on the Bio-PolyTec project (http://www.biopolytec.eu). The aim of this project is to develop novel technologies for monitoring and soft-sensing of properties of bioresorbable polymers during processing. It is hoped this will improve commercialisation by reducing processing and scrap related costs. Specifically the use of NIR and UV-Vis spectroscopies are being investigated for their ability to identify undesirable degradation products before they become problematic. This involves the use of molecular modelling and quantum dynamics as well as the use of chemometric methodology to create predictive classification and quantitative models.

Qualifications

Sept 2009 - Dec 2013; Ph.D - University of Bradford, UK

The main areas of study were undertaken as a part of this project:
1. Investigation of interactions between organic species of astrobiological relevance and inorganic mineral matrices of relevance to Mars. Studies include: surface adsorption, habit modifications, and polymorph control. Products of this study have been characterised using vibrational spectroscopy, PXRD, SEM and thermal analysis
2. Investigation of the persistence of organic species within the layered structure of clays. This has relevance within remote planetary exploration, these clays can provide a refuge for biomarker species and thus allow them to survive extreme conditions. Methods for their detection have been evaluated and developed. This has included instrumental development in vibrational spectroscopy and PXRD
3. Method development based around the use of surface enhanced Raman spectroscopy (SERS) to detect and characterise trace amounts of surface immobilised organic materials
4. Development of a chemometric methodology to simplify the analysis of large datasets, allowing relevant information to be removed quickly and effectively

Sept 2005 - July 2009; MChem(Hons) with Distinction - University of Bradford, UK

The project focused on the investigation of novel ternary co-crystal systems incorporating organic and ionic coformers using a variety of solvent systems and crystallisation methods. Products were characterised using vibrational spectroscopy (IR and Raman), PXRD and thermal analysis; and structural determination was carried out using both X-ray diffraction (SCD) methods. Seven new binary and ternary co-crystal systems were isolated and five were structurally determined by SCD analysis
